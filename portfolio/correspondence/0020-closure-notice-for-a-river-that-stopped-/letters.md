**Form:** Standard Citizen Communication Template 6A

Dear Ms. Hallgren,

Thank you for your correspondence dated 22 May 2025 concerning water levels in the Rössel.

We have registered your observation under case reference WV-2025-0477. Our records indicate that the Rössel (designation: Watercourse 47-V) is monitored via automated gauge station GS-R47V at the Fengersfors road bridge, approximately 1.2 km downstream from your property boundary at Tallstigen 14.

Current gauge data shows mean daily discharge of 0.82 m³/s against a seasonal norm of 1.4 m³/s (2015–2024 average). While this represents a 41% reduction, such variation falls within expected parameters for the early summer period. Precipitation for April–May 2025 was 38.6 mm below the ten-year mean, which accounts satisfactorily for the reduced flow you have observed.

We note your description of "parts of the riverbed visible for the first time in thirty years." Seasonal low-flow events of this character, while perhaps uncommon in recent memory, are well-documented in the historical record. The drought summer of 1994 saw discharge at GS-R47V fall to 0.31 m³/s without lasting ecological impact.

We will continue to monitor the situation via automated systems. No additional action is required on your part at this time.

Sincerely,

**Lars Nykvist**

**LETTER 3**

**To:** Sigrid Hallgren, Tallstigen 14, 612 42 Fengersfors
**From:** Department of Water Resources, Värmillen Municipality
**Regarding:** Field inspection results — Watercourse 47-V
**Form:** Field Report Template F-12

Dear Ms. Hallgren,

Following your correspondence of 14 July and 30 July, a field inspection of Watercourse 47-V was conducted on 4 August 2025 by Water Resource Officer Mikael Strand and Technical Specialist Karin Folkesson.

The inspection covered the reach from the Fengersfors road bridge (GS-R47V) upstream to the property boundary of Tallstigen 14 and approximately 800 m beyond.

**Key findings:**

- Discharge at GS-R47V: 0.09 m³/s (nominal)
- Watercourse bed fully exposed for approximately 65% of inspected reach
- Remaining surface water present as isolated pools, not contiguous flow
- No active tributary inputs observed within inspected reach
- Mineral deposits, siltation, and benthic substrate consistent with prolonged low-flow conditions

Regarding your specific observations:

1. *"Something wrong with the remaining pools"* — Water samples were taken from three isolated pools. Results indicate elevated conductivity (847 µS/cm against a background of 180–220 µS/cm), depressed dissolved oxygen (3.2 mg/L), and water temperature of 28.1°C despite ambient air temperature of 21°C. These values are anomalous but not, in themselves, indicative of a specific contamination source. We are investigating possible groundwater interaction with deeper geological formations.

2. *"Movement in the pools when nothing is stirring them"* — No biotic activity was observed during the inspection. Surface disturbances you describe may be attributable to gas ebullition from anaerobic sediment decomposition, which is consistent with the elevated temperature and depressed oxygen readings noted above.

3. *"Smell"* — Officers Strand and Folkesson did not note any unusual odor.

A further inspection is scheduled for early September. In the interim, we advise against recreating in or near the remaining pools, as a precaution.

Sincerely,

**Lars Nykvist**
Senior Water Resource Officer
Department of Water Resources

It has come to our attention that on or about 8 September 2025, you entered the channel of Watercourse 47-V at a point adjacent to your property, Tallstigen 14, and conducted unsanctioned activities including the placement of unspecified objects into the remaining water.

Under Värmillen Municipality Water Resources Bylaw (2021:12), §7, the beds and banks of watercourses designated as municipal water resources are protected areas. Unauthorized access, modification, or interference is prohibited.

We note your claim that you were "measuring the pools." Please be advised that hydrological monitoring is the responsibility of this Department. Private citizens do not possess the training or calibrated instruments to produce meaningful data.

Regarding your assertion that the water level in the pools has *increased* despite continued drought conditions: automated gauge GS-R47V continues to record zero discharge. There is no hydrological basis for localized water level increase in the absence of tributary input or precipitation.

We must ask that you refrain from further access to the watercourse channel. Continued unauthorized intervention may necessitate formal action under the bylaw referenced above.

**Date:** 12 November 2025

**To:** Sigrid Hallgren, Tallstigen 14, 612 42 Fengersfors

**Regarding:** Status update — Watercourse 47-V investigation
**Form:** Internal Review Communication Template IRC-1

Dear Ms. Hallgren,

Following the incident reported to this office on 19 October (your correspondence ref. "the pools are *growing*"), a comprehensive survey of Watercourse 47-V was undertaken between 22 October and 6 November.

The scope of the survey was expanded to include LiDAR mapping, geological resistivity profiling, and extended chemical sampling. The preliminary findings are as follows:

**Physical observations:**
- Watercourse channel now contains standing liquid in approximately 78% of its length, despite continued zero precipitation and zero recorded discharge at GS-R47V.
- Liquid is not consistent with expected composition. Field observations note: high viscosity, amber-gray coloration, surface tension estimated at 2–3× expected values. Temperature: 31–34°C at all measurement points, without thermal source.
- Liquid appears to be *entering* the channel from the substrate rather than flowing from any surface source.

**Chemical analysis (preliminary):**
- pH: unmeasurable (probe malfunction, three units)
- Conductivity: exceeds instrument range (>2000 µS/cm)
- Dissolved oxygen: 0.0 mg/L (anoxic)
- Presence of unidentified organic compounds — samples forwarded to Swedish University of Agricultural Sciences (SLU) for advanced analysis

**Notable:**
The survey team reports that isolated pools observed in August have merged into a contiguous body occupying the full channel profile. The liquid surface is described as "smooth" and "almost gelatinous." No current is detectable, but the level continues to rise at approximately 1.5 cm/day.

We wish to acknowledge that your observations over the preceding months have been, in retrospect, substantially accurate. We apologize for any impression to the contrary.

At this time, we must reiterate and strengthen our earlier advisory: **do not approach, touch, or otherwise interact with the liquid in Watercourse 47-V.** A barrier fence has been erected along the municipal right-of-way adjacent to your property.

Please direct all further inquiries to the undersigned.

Sincerely,

**Lars Nykvist**

Dear Ms. Hallgren,

This communication is intended to provide you with a status update regarding conditions at Watercourse 47-V, adjacent to your property. We appreciate your continued cooperation in refraining from access to the channel and fenced areas.

Since our last correspondence, the following developments have occurred:

The substance (the Department is currently using the provisional designation *anomalous fluid*, ref. AF-47V) now occupies the full length of the Watercourse 47-V channel, from the known headwater area northwest of your property to the confluence with Lake Värmillen approximately 3.7 km downstream. Depth is approximately 0.8 m in the central channel, which corresponds to approximately 60% of bankfull capacity.

Level continues to rise. Rate of increase has accelerated to approximately 2.3 cm/day. No source has been identified. Geological surveys confirm that the fluid is not entering from any underground spring, pipe, or fissure. It appears to be *self-generating* within the channel substrate itself.

The SLU analysis has been inconclusive. We attach the following summary:

> *The submitted samples present a composition that does not correspond to any known naturally occurring fluid. The organic fraction contains compounds not represented in available spectrographic databases. Repeated analyses produce inconsistent results. The samples exhibit thermodynamic properties inconsistent with equilibrium chemistry. We are unable to classify this material and recommend referral to additional university partners and the Swedish National Forensic Centre.*

Temperature of AF-47V remains at 33–36°C despite ambient air temperature of −8°C.

We wish to call your attention to the following:

1. The barrier fence has been extended to include a 50 m buffer on both banks.
2. A monitoring station has been established near your property, staffed continuously.
3. You may observe increased vehicle traffic and personnel near your property. These are Department staff and contractors.
4. The road adjacent to Watercourse 47-V (Fengersforsvägen) has been closed to through traffic between km markers 3.1 and 5.4.

Your cooperation and discretion are appreciated. Please direct any concerns to the undersigned.

Sincerely,

Dear Ms. Hallgren,

We acknowledge receipt of your letter dated 3 March 2026, delivered by hand to the monitoring station.

We note the contents with concern.

Specifically, we note your assertions that:

(a) the fluid in the channel has risen above the barrier fence in at least two locations adjacent to your property,

(b) the fluid appears to "move independently," including descriptions of movement against the natural gradient and apparent responses to your presence when you are near the fence,

(c) you observed "formations" or "shapes" within the fluid that were not present moments before,

(d) the fluid was "warm through the fence posts" and that vegetation within the buffer zone has begun to "grow differently," and

(e) you hear sounds from the channel at night.

We wish to address each of these in turn:

(a) The barrier fence was not designed as a containment barrier for fluid. It is a perimeter demarcation. Rising fluid levels are consistent with the observed rate of increase and do not indicate any change in the behavior of AF-47V.

(b) No movement of AF-47V has been recorded by monitoring instruments. Time-lapse cameras operating at 1-minute intervals show a static surface. We believe apparent movement may be attributable to optical effects (light refraction across a viscous, non-uniform surface) and psychological factors associated with prolonged proximity to the incident zone.

(c) See (b) above.

(d) We have observed accelerated plant growth within the buffer zone and are investigating. No conclusions are available at this time.

(e) Audio monitoring equipment deployed at the monitoring station has not recorded any anomalous sounds.

We must reiterate, in the strongest possible terms, that you should maintain distance from the fence line. If you believe the fluid has breached the fence, please contact the monitoring station immediately by telephone and do not approach the area.

We note that your correspondence was not submitted through established channels. Please direct all future communications to the Incident Coordination Team at the address below.

Regards,

Dear Ms. Hallgren,

My name is Erik Folkesson. You don't know me. I was assigned to the monitoring station near your property in April. I'm the one who sits in the van with the instruments all night.

I'm writing this outside of channels. I'm not supposed to be doing this. If anyone finds out I'll probably lose my job and maybe worse. But I've been there for four months and I need to tell someone who's *seen* it. Not someone in Stockholm reading reports. Someone who was close to it.

I've read all your letters. They're in the case file. You were right. About all of it. The movement, the sounds, the way it responds to things. The official line is instrumentation error and optical effects. It isn't.

I'm going to tell you what I've observed. Not what the instruments recorded. What *I* observed. Me, alone, at 3 AM, with no camera running. And you can do with it what you want.

The fluid moves when no one is looking. I don't mean when we're not watching the cameras. I mean the instruments record a static surface every single minute of every hour. But I started going out there *between* the minutes. At 30 seconds past the minute, when the camera is cycling. And it's moving. Slow, but moving. Rising and falling like breathing. Not randomly. There's a rhythm to it.

When I stand at the edge, it rises. Toward me. When I step back, it settles. Not always. But enough.

I'm telling you this because you lived next to it for months before they evacuated you, and I believe you saw what I'm seeing, and I believe they told you that you didn't.

Ms. Hallgren, I don't know what this is. I have a degree in environmental analysis. I've tested it forty times. Every test gives a different result. The fluid contains organic compounds that we cannot identify, produced by no known organism. It maintains a temperature of 34°C regardless of conditions. The plant growth around it is — I don't have another word — *exuberant*. The grass near the channel is a meter tall. The trees are bending toward it.

And the sound.

I know they said the audio equipment recorded nothing. They're lying or they're not listening properly. The sound is there. Below the threshold of the instruments. In the early hours, 2 or 3 AM, I can hear it from the van. A low, rhythmic sound. Not mechanical. Not geological. It sounds like something breathing. And every week it's a little louder.

Last week I went out at 3 AM and stood at the edge. I know I'm not supposed to. I just — I needed to be close to it. To understand what I'm dealing with.

It was warm. The air above it. Like standing over a heating vent. And the surface was moving. Gently. Toward me. The whole surface of the fluid, tilted in my direction, the way water tilts in a glass when you tilt the glass. But nothing was tilting the channel.

I stood there for about two minutes, and I swear to you, Ms. Hallgren, a shape rose from the center. Just a bulge at first. And then it opened. I don't mean opened like a flower or a mouth. I mean it opened the way an eye opens. And I felt — this is the part that's hard to write — I felt *seen*. Not watched. *Seen.* The way you feel when another mind recognizes you.

I ran. I've never told anyone on the team.

I'm telling you because you wrote to them, again and again, and they replied in templates and form letters, and I know how that feels. I know what it is to see something that doesn't fit in any box on any form and to be told that your boxes must be wrong.

Something is in the Rössel. I don't know if it *came* from somewhere or if it was always there, under the water, and the drought just revealed it. I don't know what it wants. But it's not leaving. It was here before the river. It'll be here after.

They're going to issue another evacuation. A wider one. I've seen the drafts. They're talking about 2 km from the channel. Maybe more. They won't say why. They'll say "continued deterioration of conditions" and "precautionary measures."

I think they're afraid. I think they don't have a box for this on any form, and they're going to keep making the exclusion zone bigger and bigger until there's nothing left to exclude.

You should know that your property — your house — the last inspection shows the fluid is at your garden wall. The garden, your garden, is the most accelerated growth zone on the entire watercourse. The plants there are — I saw the photographs. I can't describe them properly. They're beautiful. They're the most beautiful things I've ever seen. And they're wrong. They're not like anything that should be growing in Sweden.

I don't know if you'll ever be able to go home.

I'm sorry. I'm sorry we didn't listen to you when it would have mattered. I'm sorry about the templates and the form letters and the official language that has no room for what's actually happening.

I go back on shift tomorrow night. I'll be at the monitoring station. Watching the instruments that can't see what I see. Recording the measurements that don't mean anything.

I'll be standing at the edge again. Between the minutes. Where it can see me.

I think it likes being seen.

Yours,

Erik Folkesson
