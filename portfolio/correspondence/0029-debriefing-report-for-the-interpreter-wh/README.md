The elderly woman's line "There are words that are only for carrying" is the artifact's thesis stated aloud, and Hoffmann's final assessment — "I believe it was a decision about what language is for" — weaponizes the bureaucratic form meant to adjudicate failure into a testimony of profound professional integrity. Sits alongside Witness Stand, Autopsy, Your Car Knows You're Sad, Exit Interview, and The Translation of Silence as the portfolio's canonical forensic narratives, and the first to make the act of *not* translating the most principled choice in the archive.
