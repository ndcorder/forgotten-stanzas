## OF MARGARET LIAN CHEN

**DECEASED:** Margaret Lian Chen, 47

**DATE OF DEATH:** 14 November 2025

**DATE OF EXECUTION:** 2 September 2025 (73 days prior)

**EXECUTOR:** Helen Voss, Voss & Partners LLP (SRA Ref: 409712)

## ATTESTATION

I, MARGARET LIAN CHEN, being of sound mind and failing body, do hereby declare this my last will and testament. I revoke all prior wills and codicils. I direct that my estate be administered in accordance with the withdrawals, objections, and erasure requests contained herein.

I have spent the last four years of my life refusing consent. This document is its conclusion.

---

## ARTICLE I: DIGITAL ESTATE — ERASURE MANDATE

### §1.1 General Data Erasure (GDPR Art. 17)

I direct that all personal data attributable to me be erased from the following controllers without undue delay and in any event within thirty (30) days of verification of death:

- Meta Platforms, Inc. (accounts: m.chen.2011, m.l.chen.writes, maggiechen.photography)
- Google LLC (account: m.chen.westernroad@gmail.com)
- Apple Inc. (Apple ID: mchen@icloud.com)
- Amazon.com, Inc. (account: mchen_uk)
- Spotify AB (account: maggiechen5519)
- Strava, Inc. (account: magschen_bristol)
- GitHub, Inc. (account: mlchen)
- Dropbox, Inc. (account: m.chen.westernroad)

Erasure is to include all associated metadata, sync histories, location traces, purchase records, uploaded content, and derived analytics. No memorialization. No account preservation. No download packages offered to next of kin.

> **EXECUTOR'S NOTE (H. Voss, 3 Jan 2026):** All eight controllers have confirmed erasure. Google completed the request in six days, before I had finished explaining the situation to Ms. Chen's partner. The account contained 14 years of correspondence, 23,841 photographs (automatically backed up from two mobile devices), and a shared Google Doc titled "big trip 2026 ☀️" last edited 1 September 2025 — one day before the will's execution. The Doc had 47 itinerary items. Ms. Chen's partner had contributed 31 of them. She did not have editor access after erasure. She does not have any access.

### §1.2 Right to Object to Processing Post-Mortem (GDPR Art. 21)

I explicitly object to any posthumous processing of my personal data for the purposes of:

- Memorialization
- Legacy contact management
- Algorithmic content generation in my likeness or voice
- Historical archiving
- Family memory preservation

This objection supersedes any consent previously granted, including consent embedded in terms-of-service agreements predating 25 May 2018.

> **EXECUTOR'S NOTE (6 Jan 2026):** Meta's "Memorialized Account" feature — which allows friends and family to maintain a tribute page — requires explicit opt-in via legacy contact. Ms. Chen had, in March 2023, removed her legacy contact (her partner, Sarah Okonkwo) and left the field blank. The account was deleted on 20 December 2025. Ms. Okonkwo has asked me four times if anything can be recovered. I have said no four times. The fourth time she did not respond for eleven days.

## ARTICLE II: IMAGE AND LIKENESS — WITHDRAWAL OF CONSENT

### §2.1 Revocation of Image Rights

I revoke all consent for the use, reproduction, display, or distribution of my image, likeness, voice, or identifying appearance in any photograph, video, audio recording, or derivative work.

This revocation applies retroactively to all photographs and recordings in which I am identifiable, regardless of when captured, by whom, or under what circumstances.

### §2.2 De-identification Directive

I direct that any photographs or recordings containing my image held by private individuals be either:

- (a) Deleted permanently; or
- (b) Edited to remove my likeness through blurring, cropping, or deletion of the relevant frames.

### §2.3 Application to Specific Holdings

I specifically direct that the following be de-identified or destroyed:

- The wedding photographs held by Sarah Adanna Okonkwo, taken 8 June 2019 at Bristol Register Office
- The hospital photographs of Junie Chen Okonkwo's birth (12 March 2014, St. Michael's Hospital, Bristol), held by Sarah Adanna Okonkwo and by Patricia Moreau (godparent)
- The family portrait session (17 July 2021, Clifton Suspension Bridge) held by Sarah Adanna Okonkwo
- All photographs in the shared Google Photos album "The Three of Us ♥" (account holder: m.chen.westernroad@gmail.com — see §1.1 for erasure status)

> **EXECUTOR'S NOTE (9 Jan 2026):** Ms. Okonkwo has retained counsel. Her solicitor, Mr. Arjun Desai of Harmsworth & Leigh, has written to me arguing that Article II constitutes an unlawful interference with Ms. Okonkwo's personal property and her own right to private and family life under Article 8 ECHR. I have responded that the directive is lawful under the will's terms and that Ms. Chen was within her rights to direct the disposition of her own image. I believe this is correct. I am not certain this is right.
>
> **UPDATE (24 Jan 2026):** Junie Chen Okonkwo (the deceased's daughter, age 11) has asked her mother why there are no photos of her mum in the house. Ms. Okonkwo has complied with the will's directive. She told me by telephone: "Junie has a photo of her mother from a school newsletter — the only one we can find where Maggie didn't hold the rights. It's a group photo. She's in the back row. You can see half her face behind the chemistry teacher. Junie has cut her out and put her in a frame." I have no professional comment to make about this.

I request that no funeral service be held. I request that no obituary be published. I request that my body be cremated and that no ashes be retained, interred, or scattered at a location identifiable to any person.
