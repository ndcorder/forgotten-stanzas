I. Forensic Note
MEMORANDUM — CONFIDENTIAL Filed: 2019-08-12

We were engaged by Resonance Fields LLC on August 2, 2019, to investigate a reported discrepancy in attendance data from their annual music festival held July 14, 2019, at the Measured Mile venue, rural Divide County, North Dakota. The client's internal systems flagged an 11-second discontinuity across multiple independent data streams centered at 21:47:03 CDT.

Our mandate was to identify the root cause — presumed hardware fault, network dropout, or database synchronization error — and recommend corrective infrastructure.

The anomaly is not a glitch.

It is present in six independent data systems — wristband NFC taps, cell tower handoff logs, point-of-sale transaction records, portable facility occupancy sensors, venue ambient audio levels, and security camera motion detection — all of which corroborate the same 11-second period of uniform inactivity beginning at 21:47:03 and terminating at 21:47:14. During this window, 2,341 attendees on-site ceased all detectable movement simultaneously. No device registered a transaction. No sensor registered a handoff. No camera registered motion. No audio was captured.

The event left no witnesses. Exit surveys, social media sentiment analysis, and follow-up interviews conducted by the client confirm zero retrospective awareness among attendees.

Below are the relevant datasets. The data speaks for itself.
