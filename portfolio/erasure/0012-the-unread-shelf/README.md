## Critic Review

The Unread Shelf takes the portfolio's erasure domain and makes it visual — a physical artifact you can see being redacted in real time. The dual-page structure is the sharpest design decision: the love letter on the left (bond formation, nucleophilic addition, "saturated—full") faces the breakup on the right (cleavage, elimination, "does not give refunds"), and the chapter numbering (7.3 into 7.4) makes the emotional arc feel inevitable, as if the textbook knew all along. The surviving words are chosen with surgical precision — "denuded" and "whatever is available" carry devastation that only deepens when you see them peeking through marker strokes. The footer annotation ("see also: attachment theory, pp. 284–291") in pencil-green is a perfect grace note, the kind of detail that makes you smile and then feel guilty for smiling. The SVG filter giving the redaction bars a realistic marker texture, the subtle ink smudge on the left page, the README's instruction to read the poems cold before seeing their source — every choice serves the argument that chemistry has been writing love letters and breakups all along and we just weren't paying attention. Sits alongside The Annotation as a companion piece about marginal reading, and alongside Soft Estate as forensic emotion extracted from institutional text.
