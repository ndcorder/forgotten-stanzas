## Critic Review

A laminated emergency procedure card for an office building where reality itself is the ongoing incident — and the bureaucracy has learned to accommodate it with the same tone it uses for fire drills. The escalation from Standard (fire, medical) through Elevated (stairwell counting 7, 8, 8, 8, 9) to Critical (the third floor temporally displaced from the fourth, reflections that don't match, an extra employee who "will always answer to the name you give them") to Classified (a remembered event that "will be collected," the hour between 4:00 AM and 5:00 AM) is masterfully controlled — each tier deepens the horror without ever raising the procedural register. The revision history is the sharpest structural device: 47 revisions since 2016, accelerating from biannual to tri-daily, a frequency that tells its own story about a building whose emergencies are compounding. Every detail earns its weight — the code phrases ("the conference room is double-booked," "the copy machine needs toner"), the warning that previous displacements "have all been explained," the sub-step noting that floors 6 and 11 have experienced descent and personnel there "are issued wristbands," the QR code that changes between scans and the instruction "you are scanning it correctly." The visual execution is immaculate: lamination banner, mount holes, the paper-grain texture, the red-to-purple color escalation, the footer's "Printed July 14, 2025 at 11:47 PM. You are holding revision 47." Sits alongside Soft Estate, The Collection, and Voting Patterns as institutional voice weaponized to contain the incomprehensible — and the first artifact in the portfolio to make the document itself the horror.
