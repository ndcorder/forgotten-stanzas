Emergency Procedures

Meridian Tower · 600 Grenville Street
MT-EP-2016-047
Effective: Tuesday, July 15, 2025 Supersedes: Rev. 46 (July 12, 2025) FOR POSTING — ALL FLOORS
● Standard Emergency Protocols
EP-01
Fire / Smoke Detected
Activate nearest pull station. Evacuate via posted routes. Do not use elevators. Assemble at Rally Point C (parking structure P2, level 3). Do not re-enter until authorized by Fire Safety Officer.
EP-02
Medical Emergency
Call Security at ext. 4400. Administer first aid only if trained. AED units located at each elevator lobby and loading dock. Provide arriving paramedics with completed Medical Information Card MT-MIC-03.
EP-03
Earthquake / Structural Event
Drop, cover, hold. After shaking stops, proceed to nearest stairwell. Account for all personnel using floor checklist. Report missing persons and any new cracks, standing water, or odor of almonds to Security.
EP-04
Extended Power Loss
Emergency generators activate within 8 seconds. If corridor lights remain off after 30 seconds, proceed to stairwell using personal flashlight. Do not count floor numbers aloud. Remain calm.
● Elevated Emergency Protocols
EP-05
Stairwell Enumeration Irregularity
If you notice the stairwell counting floors in a non-sequential pattern (e.g., 7, 8, 8, 8, 9) or skipping a number you expected, stop descending immediately. Remain on the current landing. Press and hold the call button for 3 seconds, release for 2 seconds, press again for 3 seconds. Do not proceed until the floor indicator matches your expected floor.
If the repeated number is your destination floor, continue descending normally. The irregularity has been noted.
EP-06
Elevator Anomalous Behavior
If an elevator stops at a floor that does not correspond to any floor of Meridian Tower — including floors labeled with symbols, unrecognized numerals, or names of places you do not remember existing — do not exit. Press and hold the DOOR CLOSE button. The car will resume normal operation within 90 seconds. Do not make eye contact with anyone on the anomalous floor.
If the doors open and the corridor is filled with water, remain calm. The water is not wet. Close your eyes until the doors close.
EP-07
Unexplained Auditory Events
Persistent humming, whispering, or a sound described by witnesses as "a song played at the wrong speed" should be reported to Security via text message to 555-0147. Do not attempt to record the sound. Do not describe the sound to colleagues. Describe it only to the Security operator using the code phrase "the conference room is double-booked."
EP-08
Non-Vertical Structural Displacement
If a wall, floor, or ceiling appears to have moved to a location that does not match the building's architectural plans, note the displacement on the nearest copy of Form MT-ARCH-09 (located in each floor's safety binder). Do not push, pull, or lean on the displaced surface. Security will verify within 4 hours. Previous displacements have all been explained.
● Critical Emergency Protocols
EP-09
Temporal Floor Displacement (3rd / 4th Floor)
In the event the third floor becomes temporally displaced from the fourth — you will know this has occurred when the stairwell between these floors takes more than 30 seconds to traverse — do not attempt to reach either floor. Alert all personnel on both floors via the intercom (press INTERCOM, then dial **39). Evacuate floors 2 through 5 inclusive. The floors will be reunited within the building. This is normal and has happened before.
If you are already on the third floor and it has been displaced, remain at your desk. The building will find you. Do not look out the windows, as the view will not be correct.
EP-10
Mirror / Reflective Surface Anomaly
If your reflection does not match your current posture, expression, or clothing, or if your reflection is absent entirely: cover the surface immediately. Do not interact with it. Do not respond if the reflection speaks. Notify Floor Warden. A mirror response team will be dispatched within 15 minutes. Shaving mirrors, phone screens, elevator doors, and restroom surfaces are all considered reflective.
EP-11
Additional Employee on Floor
If a headcount reveals more personnel than are assigned to your floor, do not attempt to identify the extra individual. This is not a security breach. Complete the headcount as normal and report the total number, including the additional person, to Security. The additional individual will depart during the next headcount interval. They are cooperative. They do not need to be acknowledged.
The additional employee will always answer to the name you give them. This is expected.
EP-12
Sky Discrepancy (Windows Facing North, Floors 9–14)
If the sky visible through north-facing windows on floors 9 through 14 does not match conditions reported by local weather services — including but not limited to: wrong time of day, wrong season, unfamiliar celestial bodies, or a sky that is a color other than blue, grey, or black — lower all blinds on the affected floor and contact Security. Do not photograph the sky. The sky you are seeing is accurate for a location that is not here. It will synchronize.
EP-13
Room That Should Not Exist
If you discover a room, corridor, or space that does not appear on any floor plan and cannot be accounted for by the building's known dimensions, do not enter. Note the door's material, color, and any signage. Close the door firmly. Place a piece of furniture in front of it if possible. Report to Security with the code phrase "the copy machine needs toner." The room will be removed from the floor plan within one business day.
● Classified Emergency Protocols
EP-14
Remembered Event Protocol
If you remember an emergency that, upon checking with colleagues or records, does not appear to have occurred, do not attempt to verify the memory independently. Report to Floor Warden using the phrase "I think I attended that meeting." You will be given a blue badge. Wear it until the end of the business day. The memory is not yours. It will be collected.
EP-15
Descending Floor Protocol
If a floor begins to descend through the building without mechanical assistance — you will feel a slow, steady downward motion and may notice the ceiling of the floor below rising into your floor's space — instruct all personnel to move to the center of the floor plate. The floor will stop at the lobby. When it stops, exit through the lobby doors normally. The floor will return to its assigned position overnight.
Floors 6 and 11 have experienced this. Personnel on those floors are issued wristbands. If you are issued a wristband, wear it.
EP-16
Visitor Who Arrived Before You Left
If a visitor arrives at your office and describes, in accurate detail, a conversation you have not yet had with them, listen carefully. Do not complete the conversation as described. Vary at least one substantive detail. Provide the visitor with Form MT-VISIT-07 (gold form, available at reception). The visitor will understand. They will not be offended.
EP-17
The Hour Between 4:00 AM and 5:00 AM
If you are in the building between 4:00 AM and 5:00 AM and observe that the clock has not advanced for more than three minutes, sit down in the nearest chair or on the floor. Close your eyes. The hour will pass. Do not open your eyes until you hear the HVAC system resume. If you must open your eyes, look only at your own hands. This has always been sufficient.
