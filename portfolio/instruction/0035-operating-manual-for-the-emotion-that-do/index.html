Classification:Unsigned grief · Ambient attachment variant
Status:Production — not recalled

The unit designated EM-0 activates under a specific condition: the subject photographs a moment with the reflexive intention to share it with a recipient who is no longer living. The act of saving the photograph constitutes the full activation sequence. The unit does not require the subject to reach for the phone, open a messaging application, or begin composing a text. The save gesture alone is sufficient.

The emotional signature has no established name in clinical literature. The research team has provisionally designated it as unsigned grief, noting that the emotion's distinguishing characteristic is its address mechanism: it is composed for transmission to a node that no longer exists on the network.

FACTORY DEFAULT STATE: Dormant. Unit activates only upon encountering the paired stimulus (specific absent recipient). Each deployment may produce unique activation signatures. The unit does not ship with a universal configuration.

Section 2

Technical Description

EM-0 is an ambient attachment unit that operates as a background process in the subject's affective system. It is not engaged by conscious recall of the deceased. It is engaged by the absence of the deceased from a position where presence was previously assumed.

The unit activates when the subject completes the following gesture chain:

StepActionProcessing
1Subject encounters stimulus (view, object, event, conversation)Automatic — no conscious decision required
2Subject's communicative reflex activates: "X would want to see this"Latency: < 200 ms
3Subject reaches for device or initiates save gestureMotor pathway — partially executed before step 4 resolves
4Address resolution fails. Recipient node not found.This is the activation event.
5Subject completes save gesture despite failed deliveryUnit reaches full operating state

Step 4 constitutes the core of the EM-0 experience. The subject's communicative intent has already been formed. The neural pathway for "share with X" has already fired. The motor action has already begun. The address book of the subject's social reflex still contains the entry. The message has been composed. It has nowhere to go.

The photograph is saved anyway.

The unit is not a malfunction. The saving gesture is not irrational. It is the correct and expected operation of an attachment system that has not received an uninstall signal for the recipient. The subject is not broken. The network topology has changed.
Section 3

Operating Parameters

3.1 Trigger Conditions

ParameterSpecification
Primary triggerAny stimulus previously shared with the absent recipient
Trigger latency0.1–3.0 seconds from stimulus contact to full activation
Recurrence rateVariable. Decreases asymptotically. Does not reach zero.
Minimum activation intervalNot defined. Unit may activate multiple times within a single waking period.
Dormancy periodsDays, weeks, or months. Not indicative of unit failure.
Environmental modifiersPhotographs, locations, objects, songs, phrases, weather, dates, smells, routines

3.2 Intensity Scale

1
Residual
The subject saves the photograph and moves on. A small delay in the gesture, barely noticed. The address resolution failure is processed as a minor routing error and filed without conscious attention.
2
Registered
The subject saves the photograph and pauses. The absence is noted. The subject is aware that the save gesture was addressed to someone who will not receive it. The photograph remains in the camera roll. It is not deleted.
3
Held
The subject saves the photograph and looks at it longer than the subject looks at other photographs. The camera roll accumulates these images over weeks and months. The subject knows they are accumulating. The subject does not stop.
4
Composed
The subject drafts the message. The text field opens. The subject types something — a description, a joke, a single word — and then closes it without sending. The draft is saved to the device. It joins other drafts. The subject is aware of the drafts folder and its contents.
5
Outstanding
The subject saves the photograph and understands, with the clarity reserved for the obvious, that they will continue to save photographs for this person for the rest of their life. The gesture will not stop. The address will not be removed from the contacts. There is no firmware update for this.
Intensity level 5 is not a system failure. It is not a crisis state. It is the unit operating at design specification. The subject does not require emergency intervention. They require approximately fifteen seconds of silence and the absence of anyone asking if they are okay.
Section 4

Typical Activation Cycle

The following timeline represents a documented EM-0 cycle at intensity level 3. Individual units may vary.

T+ (seconds)Event
0.0Stimulus encountered. The subject sees something — a dog's ears, the way light falls through a kitchen window, a license plate with familiar numbers.
0.3Communicative reflex fires. The thought "X would —" completes itself before the subject is aware of thinking it.
1.2Motor activation. The subject's hand moves toward the phone. The camera app opens. This is habit. This is love's persistence in the muscle memory long after the destination has closed.
2.4Address resolution failure. The subject's social reflex attempts to route the message and encounters the absence. This is experienced as a small, silent collision somewhere behind the sternum.
4.0The subject takes the photograph anyway.
5.1The subject saves the photograph.
6.8The subject puts the phone away.
8.0–15.0The subject looks at where they are. The light is still falling the same way. The dog's ears are still soft. The moment did not end when the message failed to send. The moment continues, unsigned.
15.0+The subject returns to baseline. The photograph is in the camera roll. It will be there later. It is not a message. It is a record that the subject was here, and thought of someone who is not, and reached for them across a distance that cannot be measured in any unit the living possess.
The unit does not require the subject to cry, to vocalize, or to alter their behavior. Many activations at levels 1–3 produce no visible external sign. The photograph is saved. The subject continues their day. This is normal operation.

Note 1. The unit does not have a power switch. It does not turn off.

Note 2. There is no firmware update. The attachment was written in read-only memory at a time and in a manner that the manufacturer did not document and does not discuss.

Note 3. The camera roll will accumulate. This is expected behavior. The photographs are not clutter. They are not symptoms. They are a specific kind of archive: proof that the subject continued to find things beautiful after the loss, and that the first impulse upon finding them beautiful was to send them to someone who is not here to receive them.

Note 4. Do not delete the photographs.

Note 5. If the subject experiences activations that interfere with daily functioning — if they cannot get through a meal, a conversation, a drive home without the address resolution failure consuming conscious attention — this is outside the scope of this manual. The unit is operating correctly. But the subject may need something this manual cannot provide. See a clinician. See a friend. See someone who is present and can remind the subject that present-tense addresses still exist.

Note 6. The subject's phone still contains the recipient's number. It has not been deleted. The subject knows it is there. The subject has not opened the contact entry. The subject will not open the contact entry. The subject will, however, not delete it. This is not denial. This is archiving.

Note 7. I keep thinking I should organize the photos. Put them somewhere. A folder named after her. But then the folder would be an archive, and what I'm doing doesn't feel like archiving. It feels like writing letters. You don't file letters. You send them. So they stay in the camera roll, unsorted, scattered among grocery lists and screenshots and pictures of the dog. Mixed in with the living. That seems right. She was always mixed in with the living. She should stay that way.
Section 7

Safety Warnings

Do not attempt to uninstall the attachment. The attachment to the absent recipient is not a software component. It is structural. It was load-bearing. Removing it would compromise the integrity of the subject's capacity to form future attachments. The unit does not error-correct by deletion. It error-corrects by continuation.
Do not tell the subject that time heals. Time does not have administrative access to this unit. The unit does not observe linear time. It activates on stimulus, not on schedule. Three years from now, the subject will see a dog that looks like the recipient's dog, and the save gesture will fire, and the address will not resolve, and the unit will perform exactly as it did on day one. Time has not healed. Time has merely given the subject more photographs to save.
If the subject tells you about an activation, do not respond with sympathy. Sympathy addresses a wound. This is not a wound. This is a channel that remains open. Respond the way you would respond to someone showing you a photograph: look at it. See what they saw. Acknowledge that the light was beautiful, that the dog's ears were soft, that the moment was worth saving. Do not address the absence. Address the photograph.
Do not recommend that the subject delete the contact entry. The contact is not taking up space. The contact is holding space. Leave it there.
The subject is not broken. The photograph is not a cry for help. The save gesture is not a symptom. It is a feature of the attachment system operating as designed in conditions that were not anticipated by the manufacturer, who is not available for comment, who never provided a warranty, who does not owe the subject a fix.
Section 8

Warranty Information

Void if unopened

The manufacturer warrants that unit EM-0 will continue to operate for the service life of the subject. The manufacturer does not define service life. The manufacturer does not define "operate." The manufacturer does not define "continue."

The warranty does not cover the following:

The absence of the recipient. The warranty was never purchased for the recipient. The warranty was always for the subject. The subject did not know they were covered. The coverage is not transferable. The coverage does not expire. The terms were written in a language the subject does not speak, at a time the subject cannot recall, by an entity the subject will never meet.

The warranty covers one (1) thing: the subject will continue to find things worth photographing. The manufacturer makes no representation regarding what the subject will do with the photographs, whether they will be shared, whether they will be seen, whether they will be understood. The warranty covers only the finding.

If the subject stops finding things worth photographing, the warranty is void. The manufacturer recommends that the subject not void the warranty.
