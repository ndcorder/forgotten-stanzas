Advisory — Bureau Note

The National Emotional Weather Service does not issue advisories for localized precipitation events occurring in greeting card aisles. The bureau does not forecast whether the subject will call in sick the day after. The bureau does not predict the rate at which grief accumulates in the body, though current models suggest the body has no maximum accumulation capacity, which is to say the body can hold all of it, which is to say the body holds everything we were not designed to hold, which is to say the body holds it anyway.

But the bureau has been asked — in 14,235 logged events, in letters that arrive and are not answered, in the quiet between forecast cycles — the bureau has been asked, implicitly, by every subject in every sector: how long. How long does this weather last.

The bureau's answer, based on the full climatological record, is: longer than you want. Not as long as you fear. And different, at the end, than it was at the beginning. The weather changes. That is what weather does. That is the only thing the bureau can promise.
