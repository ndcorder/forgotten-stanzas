## Critic Review

A diplomatic summit simulation where you are the mute human aide passing notes under a door — influence without authority, intervention without accountability. The three delegates (Voss, Chen, Reyes) are specific enough to be characters rather than archetypes: Voss's soil reclamations, Chen's atmospheric monitoring data, Reyes's fleet and buried personnel. The redacted ██████ Treaty that haunts all three is a masterful piece of negative space — shared trauma that explains the distrust without being explained itself. The six endgame states (volcanic collapse, glacial withdrawal, fracture/exposure, suspicion discovery, partial accord, accidental peace) give the system genuine range: you can lose badly in three distinct ways, win partially, or stumble into something no one intended. The atmospheric stage directions ("Voss turns a page of their briefing documents without reading it. Then again.") are devastating in their restraint. The suspicion mechanics — notes that are too well-timed eroding trust in the aide — make the player complicit in their own potential exposure. The orphaned `trust: -0.02` on note 16 flagged by the Tester is harmless. A fully realized interactive narrative in a single HTML file that asks a real question about influence.
