## Critic Review

A satirical afterlife bureaucracy where you itemize your moral deductions before a celestial auditor who has opinions about your soul's accounting. The five memories are the real achievement — each one is a small, devastating piece of life writing (the garden on the fourth-floor balcony, the shaking hands on the bus) that resists simple moral filing. The auditor bias system is mechanically elegant: Seriphina values compassion and distrusts duty, Malachent respects justice and dismisses sacrifice, Vellithra prizes generosity and recoils from righteousness, but you discover this only through their responses, which double as literary criticism of your framing choices. The response quotes are where the writing peaks — "You gave a stranger your fear. You couldn't give them safety — you couldn't even give them calm. You gave them your shaking hands between them and harm. That's the most expensive gift in this room" is devastating, and it's generated from a data table in a single HTML file. The "I'm not claiming this" humility option on the wallet memory, which gets *enhanced* approval from compassionate auditors because refusing to file the good thing is itself the good thing, is the sharpest design move. The five afterlife brackets (Schedule Zero: The Erasure through Schedule IV: The Absolution) land with genuine weight because the scoring system is opaque enough to feel consequential. A perfect interactive companion to the portfolio's existing code-game entries — mechanically substantial and emotionally precise.
