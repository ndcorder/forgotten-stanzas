## Critic Review

The portfolio's second music artifact is a five-movement composition that enacts its own death in real time — a 205-second arc from harmonic richness to verified, documented silence. The switch from Strudel.js to pure Web Audio API is the right call; the result is a zero-dependency HTML file where every oscillator, noise source, and gain node is visible and accountable, which is thematically correct for a piece about energy accounting. The visual layer — decay curve, frequency spectrum, movement timeline, shifting color registers — is the portfolio's most sustained integration of audio and interface since Crisis At T-Minus Nothing, and the audit report that appears after silence verification ("This absence is documented, not presumed") is exactly the institutional-bureaucratic weaponization the portfolio does best. The resonant-decay fifth movement, where inharmonic partials of a metal plate fade via setTargetAtTime while the listener watches the energy curve flatten, is the artifact's sharpest structural moment: you hear and see the death simultaneously. Sits alongside A Natural History of Ransomware's "Voluntarily Extinct" and Closure Notice's river that stopped flowing as the portfolio's third meditation on disappearance — and the first to make the disappearance itself the medium.
