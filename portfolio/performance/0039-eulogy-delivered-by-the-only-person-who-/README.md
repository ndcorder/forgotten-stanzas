## Critic Review

The portfolio's canonical performance piece and its most emotionally devastating artifact since Operating Manual. The central structural inversion — a performance score whose most important direction is who isn't there — makes the form itself the argument: institutional stagecraft precision applied to a eulogy for someone who made other people's invisible deaths visible, and whose own death is witnessed only by a man who knew her as a Tuesday customer. The specific details are load-bearing at every level: the microphone set for someone taller, Sarajevo corrected gently and never forgotten, the Irish Times brought because he didn't know which country to bring, "You'll catch your death" said to a war correspondent who laughed because she'd been in worse. The heating system as second performer — indifferent mechanical rhythm running through grief — is the portfolio's finest use of environmental sound since Extinction, and the moment Gerald notices the echo ("You can hear it on the consonants") is the quietest devastation in the artifact: a man alone, speaking to someone who isn't there, realizing the walls will hold his words longer than any human in the room will, and the post-score note explicitly refusing to let this become metaphor — "The echo is the echo. It is enough." The production note specifying that audience members must never occupy the chapel seats is the artifact's thesis stated as stage direction: readers are witnesses to the emptiness, not participants in the grief, and that distinction between performance and testament is the most formally rigorous instruction in the portfolio. The CSS is immaculate — Courier Prime for mechanism, EB Garamond for speech, the warm dark palette suggesting institutional chapels and the specific loneliness of fluorescent lighting, the progress bar as a timeline the reader can't stop. Sits alongside Exit Interview, The Translation of Silence, and Curtain Speech as the portfolio's canonical performance-death works, and the first to make the audience's absence the central structural event.
