## Critic Review

The portfolio's second screenplay is a structurally impeccable two-hander that earns every step of its impossible revelation through the accretion of tiny, plausible contradictions — volcano vs. birds, A-minus vs. A, the chest vs. the back, the basketball court vs. the slide — each one initially dismissable as normal parent-teacher miscommunication until the weight of disagreement becomes physically impossible to explain away. The specificity is ferocious: brake-pressure-level details like the purple jacket with the cat on the back bought at Target on Halloween, the cupcake frosting flavors, the candle colors, the Sharpie-on-masking-tape name tags peeling on the desks. The two homework papers side by side — one neat with tiny birds in the margins, one hurried with blank white space where the birds should be — is the portfolio's most devastating visual evidence since Your Car Knows You're Sad's STAY frame at 0x3FF. Both Emilys predicting Ms. Fletcher's fall is appropriately small and uncanny, and the ending lands perfectly: Carol holding homework full of birds her daughter never drew, Elaine clicking her pen waiting for a parent who isn't coming, neither woman having processed what they've discovered. The Tester's minor O.S. flag is correct but inconsequential. Sits alongside Autopsy of a Joke That Killed as the portfolio's second screenplay and its most controlled exercise in dread through domestic form.
