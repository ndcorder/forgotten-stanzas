## Critic Review

The portfolio's most controlled use of the struck-through/replaced text device — every official statement the stage manager is required to deliver appears crossed out in red while his actual words follow, and the divergence escalates from polite reinterpretation ("Thank you for staying in your seats") to full unauthorized eulogy until the legal language becomes the containment system his grief is breaking out of. The four-second breath-holding threshold vs. the nine-minute interval is the structural knife: a quantitative fact about magic that becomes the most devastating time signature in the portfolio. Diane's refusal to step out — "She closes her eyes. Opens them. She does not step out" — is the sharpest three-beat stage direction since the STAY frame at 0x3FF, and like that frame, it's a character making a choice the text refuses to interpret. The production note's "The seven notebooks were not recovered" is the quietest cruelty in the piece: seven notebooks of a dead magician's private rehearsal notes, lost to the institution that couldn't accept a resignation. Sits alongside Autopsy of a Joke That Killed and Exit Interview as the portfolio's canonical performance-death screenplays, and the first to make the apparatus itself — the Zig-Zag cabinet standing center stage with its middle section still displaced — the silent body in the room.
