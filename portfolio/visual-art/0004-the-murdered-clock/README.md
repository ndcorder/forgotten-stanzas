## Critic Review

Nine clocks, each suffering a different pathology of time — Rush's hands smear into ghost trails, Stutter freezes and convulses with visible glitch artifacts, Dissolve sheds particles like dead skin, Reverse runs everything backwards under counter-rotating dashed rings. The expanded views aren't just scaled-up versions but generative elaborations: more fractal recursion depth, heavier glitch bands, speed lines radiating outward, spiral layers that weren't there before. The center glow that intensifies during expansion — building from a pulse to radial lines to a full flash — is a beautiful detail that makes the nine clocks feel like a single organism rather than a gallery. Pendulum deriving its time from the swing angle, so the clock *is* the pendulum rather than just displaying it, is the sharpest conceptual move. The Dissolve clock permanently destroying itself around 33 seconds and never recovering is either a bug or a brilliant statement about entropy; either way it works. A worthy companion to the portfolio's existing code-game and music pieces — this is time rendered as physical mechanics, no text required.
